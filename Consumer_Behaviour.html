Consumer Behaviour

Assignment Brief

This assignment was to evaluate a brand's appeal to Gen Z through its physical store, analysing the target segment, factors influencing behaviour, marketing mix, and conducting SWOT and PESTLE analyses. It included two primary research methods and recommendations for improved Gen Z engagement.

Personal Process

Throughout this project, I gained a solid understanding of primary research fundamentals, delving into the intricacies of conducting observations, interviews, focus groups, and surveys. I not only grasped the nuances between quantitative and qualitative data but also acquired valuable skills in creating industry-relevant SWOT and PESTLE analyses.

A highlight of the learning process was my visit to the Brandy Melville store in Covent Garden, where I conducted firsthand observations. Additionally, interviewing a classmate who frequents Brandy Melville provided insightful perspectives on consumer behaviour, reinforcing the significance of these insights in practical applications.

This experience has been instrumental in broadening my comprehension of research methodologies and their relevance in understanding consumer dynamics.

Skills Learned

Over the duration of this module I acquired a diverse set of valuable skills:

  • Primary research fundamentals
  • Proficiency in conducting SWOT and PESTLE analyses
  • Research methodology understanding
