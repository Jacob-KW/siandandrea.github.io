Experience

Language

I have been lucky enough to have been surrounded by a variety of different cultures throughout my lifetime. Coming from a vibrant blend of cultures myself - being a mix of Italian, English and Welsh - as well as growing up in the predominantly Pakistani area of Walthamstow in East London, I have always had an appreciation for the rich perspectives different cultures bring.

This was expanded upon once I began secondary school, moving to Essex in order to attend the language centred Anglo European School.

During my time at Anglo, I was able to delve into many other cultures forming friendships with people from all over the world including. Sweden, Norway, Dubai, Portugal, France, Hungary and more!

Already fluent in both English and Italian, I also had the opportunity to expand my language skills, taking lessons in German, French, Spanish and even a few taster lessons in Japanese, and Russian!

My passion for French and Spanish blossomed into immersive experiences, with exchanges in Lyon, Dinan, Blanes, and a month long exchange to Granada. Although I had to juggle keeping up with my schoolwork whilst in Granada, living in Spain for a month helped me learn Spanish in a way that was impossible to learn in lessons, helping me receive a grade 9 at GCSE!

I was lucky enough that our Sixth-form, offered the International Baccalaureate – one of only two state schools to offer it. I opted for A-IB (a blend of both a-levels as IBs), as one of my favourite subjects Drama was not offered as part of the IB. I am grateful for this choice as it allowed me to compare the two, receiving the positives for both.

Drama

Theatre and performing has always been a passion of mine since primary school. I have been lucky enough to be a part of many experiences due to this. I was a member of the International Drama Academy, I did work experience in my schools drama department, I led group rehearsals during my school plays, and I organised a special event for my schools version of the Eisteddfod.

Drama has been an extremely valuable tool, helping me with presentation skills, diction, projection, and overall confidence.

Psychology & Sociology

With both of my parents having worked as social workers for the NHS, I have been fortunate to grow up understanding in more depth why people behave the way that they do. I studied both Psychology and Sociology at A-level in order to further deepen this understanding.

As part of my Bronze DofE, I carried out some volunteering work for a Young Carers Group at my local YMCA, providing respite for young carers. This gave me a greater empathy for peoples situations which we often have no insight into.

As a result of this, I led a mental health awareness day at my school, affiliated with Young Minds, called Hello Yellow. This event raised over £100 for charities helping with youth mental health.

London & Nottingham

I chose to live in Nottingham and commute to London for university, a unique decision that reflects my inclination to avoid the easy path for excitement and enrichment (also making for great small talk!).

One of the reasons for this was because my parents and my little sister moved back to Italy as I began University, and so I decided to move in with my long term boyfriend, currently studying Computer Science at the University of Nottingham. This has been a great decision as I have had the joy of experiencing the magic and opportunities that London brings as well as the beauty and social life that Nottingham has to offer!
